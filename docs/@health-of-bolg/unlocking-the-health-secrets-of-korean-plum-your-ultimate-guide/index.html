Common Misconceptions About Consuming Green Plums And Their Extracts

Busting Myths Associated With Overconsumption

Myth 1: Overeating green plums can lead to cyanide poisoning

One of the biggest concerns people have about consuming green plums is the fear of cyanide poisoning. While it is true that plum pits contain small amounts of cyanogenic glycosides, which can release hydrogen cyanide under certain conditions, it would take an extremely large quantity of pits to cause any harm. In fact, you would need to consume around 60-70 plum pits to experience any ill effects.

Myth 2: Cooking does not remove the toxic compounds in plum pits

Contrary to popular belief, cooking thoroughly renders plum pits safe for consumption. The heat from cooking breaks down the cyanogenic glycosides and destroys hydrogen cyanide as it is not heat-stable. So rest assured that when you enjoy cooked dishes or desserts made with green plums or their extracts, there is no need to worry about potential toxicity.

Myth 3: Korean green plums are highly toxic compared to other fruits

While Korean green plums may contain trace amounts of naturally occurring toxins within their seeds like many other fruits (such as almonds), they are generally considered safe for consumption in moderation. It's important not to confuse them with poisonous plants like poison hemlock or deadly nightshade whose toxicity levels are far more dangerous than those found in fruit seeds.

Myth 4: The side effects of consuming too many green plums include convulsions and rapid heart rate

Experiencing symptoms such as dizziness, headache, nausea, rapid heart rate, or convulsions after consuming green plums is highly unlikely unless you consume an excessive amount of plum pits. A single plum pit is usually harmless and will not cause any significant health effects. So feel free to enjoy the unique flavor and nutritional benefits of green plums without worrying about these extreme side effects.

Myth 5: Green plum extracts are a magical cure-all for various health conditions

While green plum extracts may offer certain health benefits due to their rich antioxidant content and potential anti-inflammatory properties, it's important to remember that they are not a magical cure-all for every ailment. It's always advisable to consult with a healthcare professional before incorporating any new supplements or natural remedies into your routine.

Comparative Analysis: Fresh Vs Dried Vs Extracted Form

When it comes to Korean plum, also known as Maesil, there are various forms available in the market. You can find fresh plums, dried plums (prunes), and extracted plum syrup. But what are the differences between these forms? And when should you opt for which form? Let's dive into a comparative analysis of fresh vs dried vs extracted Korean plum.

Difference between fresh, dried and extracted forms

Fresh Korean Plum:

Fresh Korean plum, also called green plum or Maesil, is only available for a short time in the spring season. It is used to make the famous plum syrup or extract that has been cherished for its medicinal properties since 200 AD. The process involves washing the plums, drying them, and layering them with sugar in a jar. The mixture is left to ferment for 90 days until it transforms into a sweet and fragrant syrup.

The benefit of using fresh Korean plum lies in its natural state without any additives or processing methods involved. This ensures that you get all the nutrients and health benefits directly from the fruit itself. It retains high levels of citric acid which aids digestion by promoting saliva secretion and digestive enzyme production.

Extracted Korean Plum:

Extracted Korean plum, also known as Maesil Cheong or Maesil Aekgiss, is a syrup made from fermented green plums. This extracted form is commonly used as a sweetener in cooking and offers a distinct fragrant flavor. The process involves layering green plums and sugar in a jar, fermenting them for 90 days to develop the desired taste profile, and then removing the plums while retaining the flavorful syrup.

The advantage of using extracted plum syrup lies in its versatility as an ingredient. It can enhance the taste of various dishes such as sauces, dressings, desserts, or even beverages. Whether you're experimenting with new recipes or adding a unique twist to traditional ones, this sweet and aromatic syrup can elevate your culinary creations.
