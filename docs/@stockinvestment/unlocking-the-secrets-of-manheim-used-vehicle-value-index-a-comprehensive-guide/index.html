Understanding the Basics of Manheim Used Vehicle Value Index

The Manheim Used Vehicle Value Index is a powerful tool that provides valuable insights into the pricing trends of used cars in the automotive industry. In this section, we will delve into the definition and purpose of the index, discuss its importance in the automotive industry, and take a brief look at its history.

The Definition & Purpose of Manheim Used Vehicle Value Index

The Manheim Used Vehicle Value Index is published by Cox Automotive and tracks the prices car dealerships pay for used cars at auctions. It takes into account various factors such as car types, different mileages, and excludes heavy trucks and motorcycles while including 20 other vehicle classes. By eliminating outlier data and calculating mean average sales prices based on mileage and market class, it provides an accurate representation of pricing trends for used vehicles.

Why is the Manheim Index Important in Automotive Industry?

The significance of the Manheim Used Vehicle Value Index cannot be overstated in the automotive industry. Car dealerships heavily rely on this index to understand current pricing trends for used cars at auctions. It allows them to make informed purchasing decisions by providing insights into what they should expect to pay for used cars. This information helps dealerships buy trade-ins below auction value, enabling them to maximize profits even if they are unable to sell a car locally.

For consumers, although primarily intended for dealerships, some valuable information can still be gleaned from this index. It offers a general understanding of overall price trends for used vehicles bought by dealerships at auctions. However, it's important to note that it does not provide specific pricing information for individual vehicles at a consumer level. Consumers may find online valuation tools more helpful when estimating prices for specific cars.

A Brief History of The Manheim Used Vehicle Value Index

The history of the Manheim Used Vehicle Value Index dates back several decades. Its inception can be traced back to 1995 when Manheim, the world's largest wholesale auto auction company, began publishing this index. Over the years, it has become an industry-standard tool for car dealerships and an essential resource for understanding pricing trends in the used car market.

Since its launch, the index has undergone several changes to enhance its accuracy and relevance. It now includes a seasonal adjustment factor that takes into account changing mix and mileage over time. This adjustment ensures that the index reflects current market conditions accurately.

In recent times, the Manheim Used Vehicle Value Index has witnessed significant trends. The onset of COVID-19 led to a drastic rise in prices as global computer chip shortages disrupted manufacturing capabilities and increased demand for used cars. However, as of March 2022, there was a slight decrease of 3.3% from February 2022's reading while still remaining considerably higher at 24.8% compared to March 2021.

Impact of The COVID-19 Pandemic on Car Market as Reflected by MUVVI

The COVID-19 pandemic has had a tremendous impact on various industries across the globe, and the automotive sector is no exception. In this article, we will delve into the effects of the crisis on the car market, as seen through the lens of MUVVI, a leading data analytics company specializing in automotive trends and insights.

Crisis Effects Seen Through MUVVI's Lens

MUVVI's comprehensive analysis reveals several key effects of the COVID-19 pandemic on the car market:

  1. Decline in Sales: The pandemic led to an unprecedented decline in car sales worldwide. As countries implemented lockdowns and travel restrictions, consumers were hesitant to make significant purchases like cars. This resulted in dealerships experiencing reduced footfall and sales plummeting.

  2. Shift towards Online Purchases: With physical showrooms closing or operating at limited capacity during lockdowns, buyers turned to online platforms for their car purchasing needs. MUVVI's data indicates a surge in online searches for cars and an increase in e-commerce platforms catering to vehicle sales.

  3. Preference for Used Cars: Economic uncertainty caused many consumers to prioritize cost-saving measures during these challenging times. As a result, there was an increased demand for used cars rather than new ones due to their lower price points.

  4. Supply Chain Disruptions: The closure of manufacturing plants and disruption in supply chains due to lockdown measures impacted both new car production and availability of spare parts for repairs and maintenance.

How Resilient Was Car Market During Pandemic According to MUVVI?

Despite facing numerous challenges brought about by the pandemic, including decreased consumer spending power and disrupted supply chains, MUVVI's findings highlight some resilience within the car market:

  1. Recovery Post-Lockdowns: As countries gradually eased restrictions following initial waves of infections, the car market experienced a gradual recovery. The pent-up demand resulted in an uptick in sales, with consumers seeking personal transportation options to avoid crowded public transport.

  2. Shift towards Electric Vehicles: MUVVI's data suggests that the pandemic accelerated the shift towards electric vehicles (EVs). As environmental concerns gained prominence during this time, buyers showed increased interest in EVs due to their lower emissions and potential cost savings on fuel.

  3. Adaptation of Dealerships: Car dealerships quickly adapted to changing circumstances by implementing strict hygiene protocols, offering virtual showrooms and test drives, and providing contactless delivery services. These measures helped restore consumer confidence and drive sales amidst ongoing health concerns.

  4. Focus on Local Markets: With international travel restrictions in place, domestic tourism witnessed a surge as people explored local destinations within their countries or regions. This trend contributed to increased car rentals and purchases within local markets.

Case Study: Comparing Sedans and SUVs using MUVVI data

In today's automotive market, sedans and SUVs are two popular choices among consumers. Each has its own set of advantages and disadvantages, but which one holds up better over time? Let's dive into the Manheim Used Vehicle Value Index (MUVVI) data to compare sedans and SUVs based on recession and recovery trends.

Sedan Vs SUV - Who Wins Over Time Based On Recession And Recovery Trends?

During times of economic uncertainty, such as recessions, consumer preferences for vehicle types can shift. This is where the MUVVI data comes in handy to understand the impact on sedan and SUV prices.

According to the latest MUVVI report, in the first half of October, both sedans and SUVs saw a decline in prices compared to previous years. However, when analyzing recession periods specifically, it is interesting to note that sedans tend to perform worse than SUVs. For example:

  1. Recession Period: In 2008-2009 during the global financial crisis, sedan prices experienced a significant drop due to reduced demand. On the other hand, SUV prices were more resilient during this period.

  2. Recovery Period: As economies started recovering from recessions or crises, there was a gradual increase in demand for vehicles again. The MUVVI data shows that while sedan prices slowly recovered over time post-recession, they did not reach pre-recession levels as quickly as SUV prices did.

  3. Overall Trend: Looking at long-term trends based on various recessions throughout history indicates that despite temporary fluctuations during economic downturns, overall resale values for SUVs have been relatively stronger compared to sedans.

It's important to consider factors such as utility needs (e.g., cargo space), fuel efficiency requirements or preferences (e.g., hybrid/electric options), driving conditions (e.g., off-road capabilities), and personal preferences when choosing between sedans and SUVs. However, if you're looking for a vehicle that holds its value better during uncertain economic times, SUVs seem to have the upper hand.

While this analysis is based on historical data trends, it's worth noting that future market conditions can always vary. Economic factors, technological advancements, environmental concerns, and changing consumer preferences are all variables that can influence the performance of sedan and SUV prices over time.
