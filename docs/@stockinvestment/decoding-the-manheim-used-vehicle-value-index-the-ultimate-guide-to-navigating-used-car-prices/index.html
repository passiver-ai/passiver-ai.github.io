The Methodology Behind Calculating This Index

The Manheim Used Vehicle Value Index is developed by analyzing a vast database of over 5 million used vehicle transactions annually. Through statistical analysis, the index measures used vehicle prices independently of underlying shifts in the characteristics of vehicles being sold. This ensures that changes in price reflect actual market trends rather than fluctuations in factors such as mileage or condition.

To calculate the index, Manheim applies statistical analysis techniques to determine pricing trends across different market segments and time periods. By doing so, they can provide accurate and reliable information about wholesale used-vehicle prices.

One important aspect of this methodology is adjusting for seasonality. Market conditions may vary throughout the year due to factors like holidays or changing consumer preferences. Therefore, seasonal adjustments are made to ensure that comparisons between different time periods accurately reflect underlying price movements.
